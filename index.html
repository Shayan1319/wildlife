About KPK wildlife Department!

Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Wildlife Department which was notified as an attached Department in August, 1994, started as wing of Forest Department in 1975. The Wildlife Organization in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Province emerged in 1974 as an ex-cadre wing of the Forest Department under the administrative control of Chief Conservator of Forests. This wing functioned for 4 years as a non significant and low priority organization and was ultimately abolished in 1978 under an economy drive.Conservation of wildlife again became a priority and the wildlife wing was re-established in 1981......

Message!

It’s a moment of pride to express that in pursuance to the good governance policy of the government, the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Wildlife Department has developed Management Information System (MIS) with shared database for compilation of data into a wholesome digitized program. It is a shift from conventional record keeping to advanced technological development which will contribute to efficient, proactive and convenient working processes through improved automation. In fact, it’s the need of the current development scenario to adopt progressive approaches towards proficient use of technological means. The need for use of such approaches intensified with the passage of time and multiple technology based programs have been initiated in the government departments/ organizations. As an outcome, efficiency in task management will be achieved for evolving proactive strategies and long term planning. It will enhance ability of the department to respond to issues timely. The newly developed website, MIS with shared database and mobile app will enable the department to access the diversified information just on a click. The initiative is also intended to cater for public service delivery such as application for issuance of shooting and possession licences, etc.

Mr. Muhammad Abid Majeed
Message of Secretary FE&WD

I am pleased to express that the Wildlife Department has developed Website, MIS with shared database and mobile app under the 10 Billion Tree Tsunami Program (Wildlife Component) after an adopted mechanism for completion of assigned task. Earlier the data of the department was consolidated manually through progress reports which was cumbersome task and time consuming to extract a particular information from huge data maintained over a period of time through file record. Consequent upon deployment of the MIS with share database into the mainstream of the department, instant access will be available to the officers of the wildlife department to track progress instantly pertaining to their respective jurisdictions. Moreover, the MIS with shared database will facilitate Chief Conservator Wildlife to monitor progress available on cell phone sharply regarding field formations throughout the province as a centralized set up. The initiative will ensure improved coordination among the territorial offices of the department. The dual interface system has been developed with such provision that officers will view the reports and graphs while officials will make mandatory entries into the system. I am confident that the officers and officials concerned will show professional dedication and commitment to keep up the shared database updated. Required training has been imparted to the dealing officers/ officials of the department as an orientation into the newly developed computerized program. The MIS with shared database and mobile app will contribute to better working environment and obtain requisite data instantly.
